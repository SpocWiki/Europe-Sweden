# [[Vänern]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../../../../WikiData/WD~Vänern,173596|WD~Vänern,173596]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Vänern** ( VAY-nərn, US also  VEN-ərn, Swedish: [ˈvɛ̂ːnɛɳ]) 
> is the largest lake in the European Union 
> and the third-largest lake in Europe after Ladoga and Onega in Russia. 
> 
> It is located in the provinces of Västergötland, Dalsland, 
> and Värmland in the southwest of the country. 
> 
> With its surface located at 44 metres (144 ft) above sea level 
> and a maximum depth of 106 metres (348 ft), 
> the lowest point of the Vänern basin is 62 metres (203 ft) below sea level. 
> 
> The average depth is a more modest 27 metres (89 ft), 
> which means that the lake floor is above sea level on average.
>
> Vänern drains into Göta älv towards Gothenburg and the Kattegat opening strait 
> between the Atlantic Ocean’s North Sea and the Baltic Sea. 
> It is the only one of the ten largest lakes in Sweden not to drain out to the eastern coastline.  The Göta Canal built in the 19th century forms a waterway that runs to Vättern wholly rising when proceeding eastward, and then a navigable river linking the east coast with Vänern. The main inflow of water comes from Klarälven entering Vänern near Karlstad with its source in Trøndelag in Norway.
>
